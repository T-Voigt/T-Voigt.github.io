Why I love college

I am free
I now am able to live my life on my own terms. I can choose how I want to structure my life and what I want to do. I think this is equally as exciting as it is scary and for this reason it is one of the reasons why I love college.
The people I meet
I love meeting new people and the University of Pittsburgh is full of new people. I really enjoy talking to people, hearing their stories, and learning new things. There are so many people here that it overwhelms a lot of people but I think it is really exciting to meet new people and thats why it is another reason why I love college.
The campus
I love being in a city. Every summer when I go back to Germany I live in my grandparents‘ apartment which is in a city. I just really enjoy being close to everything and not always needing to get in a car. Because of the fact that Pitt is in the city makes me love college even more.
The clubs
Pitt offers a huge variety of clubs that students can join. I love this because I can join the ski club and go to Seven Springs often to ski, which I love doing. But I can also join a club that is academically driven and which can help me broaden my scope of what engineers do.
Sporting games
I love going to Pitt‘s sporting events like football games, and soccer games. Especially the football games because of the student section. It is an amazing experience to be a part of the student section and it is definitely another reason why I love college.
Distance from home
I really like that Pitt is still close to home because I love being with my family. I enjoy the fact that I can get dinner with them every once in a while or meet up with them on the weekends without having to go home. I didn‘t think that I would love college if it were really close to home but I actually do.

Things I have Learned About College

  1. Before I came to college I thought the classes were going to be a lot harder than they were in high school, I was right.
  2. Before coming to college I thought that it might be like my high school where everyone knew everyone. That is definitely not the case here, there are so many people here that its nearly impossible to know everyone.
  3. I learned that I was completely spoiled when it came to food. My mom cooked a healthy German dinner every night for my family. Here I have a large variety of different foods to choose from, but nothing resembles the food I ate at home.
