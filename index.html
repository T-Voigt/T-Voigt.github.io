My Bio

     I am a Freshman Engineering student at the University of Pittsburgh. H2P!! I was originally born in Germany, and I lived there for about few years. When I was 5 my family immigrated to the United States. My family moved to Murrysville, a suburb of Pittsburgh, and has lived there since. I started my education here in the US since I was not old enough to attend school in Germany at the time that we moved.
     I had a lot of trouble with my education when I started attending school. I was still in the process of learning German by the time I went to school in the US. This made it really difficult for me since I was learning two languages at the same time. My parents wanted to make sure that all of their children were still able to speak German and so they insisted that we speak German in our house. This, however, meant that I was getting confused with my languages for a while when I was starting school. I attended Mother of Sorrows for first and second grade but then from third grade on I went to my local public school, Franklin Regional. I really enjoyed my time at Franklin and did a lot of things I consider to be worth while.
     In high school I loved my art classes but also my science and math classes. I really enjoyed art because it allowed me to express myself and I was able to be as creative as I wanted to be. I took many art classes in both 3D and 2D, and I really think it was a great thing to do because they helped shape me to be the person I am today. I also really enjoyed my science classes. I took many science classes but my favorite were physics and chemistry. I also really enjoyed math, mainly because of the teachers, but I learned a lot and actually enjoyed it, too.
