AUTOMATIC BRAKING SYSTEM

     To understand the benefits of automatic emergency braking systems, one must first understand what exactly this system is. In a car that doesn‘t have this system, emergency braking is done by the driver. For example, if a car were to suddenly stop in front of me, then it is up to me to slam on the brakes to stop my car before I crashed into the car in front of me. In this situation one must consider the human reaction time to be a factor for this emergency stop to be effective. In the world that we live in, however, there are distractions everywhere and this can cause an increase in our reaction time. In order to keep people safe behind the wheel, engineers should devote time and resources to the invention of safety systems in cars. In a vehicle that has an automatic emergency braking system, the human reaction time is taken out of the equation. Reimagine the same situation as just described, but this time my car does have an automatic emergency braking system. Now my car would come to a stop sooner because my reaction time is no longer a variable in stopping time. The automatic braking system reacts for the driver and automatically applies the brakes when it senses that the car is going to crash [4].

How It Works

     Now that you know what the system is, it is important to know how it works. Each automotive manufacturer that offers automatic braking systems has their own specific system, but the setup is basically the same. The front of the car has a series of sensors that scan the area ahead of the car. These sensors could be lasers, radar, or even video data. In many automobiles the sensors can detect objects up to 250 meters away [5]. The information picked up by these sensors is processed to determine if an object is in the way of the vehicle. If there happens to be an object, the system then calculates the speed of the object in front of the car. If the speed of the object is significantly less than the car‘s speed, then the computer could determine if a collision or crash is likely. If the computer determines that a collision is likely, it is able to apply the brakes for the driver. This allows the car to significantly reduce its speed, decreasing the severity, or to even prevent the crash from happening all together. This system can save lives and reduce the amount of property damage caused from a collision.

Reducing Rear-endings

     The main benefits of an automatic emergency braking system are that it can significantly reduce the danger of an accident or prevent the accident from happening in the first place. There are many types of accidents that one can be involved in when driving, but 28% of accidents in the United States are rear-endings [6]. This means that over a quarter of accidents occur when one car fails to brake in time and ends up hitting the car in front of it. Studies determined that in 80% of rear-end accidents the drivers do not use their brakes at all or do not use the car‘s full braking capacity [5]. Automatic emergency braking systems could prevent most of these accidents from ever happening by applying the brakes for the driver and stopping the car. Automatic braking systems can also decrease the severity of high speed accidents [3]. High speed accidents are more fatal and the automatic emergency braking system would be able to reduce the speed of the car before a high speed crash significantly to help avoid worst case scenario accidents. In a test performed in 2013 by the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS) and the Highway Loss Data Institute (HLDI), the researchers tested the automatic emergency braking system of a variety of vehicles that were advertised as incorporating this kind of system and ranked them on their performance in a front-to-rear-end crash. To simulate the car being rear-ended, an inflatable model of a car was used to prevent damage to the manufacturer vehicles. The cars were then driven at a speed of 12 miles per hour in the first test and 25 miles per hour in the second test at the stationary model. What the testers found was that the systems were always able to indicate if there was an object with significantly slower speed in front of the vehicle. Also, if the vehicle had an automatic emergency braking system, this system was able to slow the cars down in every test reducing the speed of impact. For many cars the breaking system was even able to stop the car entirely and prevent a collision from occurring at all. This test proved the benefits of having an automatic emergency braking system because it can help prevent accidents or decrease the severity of accidents [7].

Increasing Pedestrian Safety

     Along with rear-ending accidents, there are also crashes that involve pedestrians. These collisions are often very dangerous for the pedestrians and can result in serious injury or death. The National Center for Statistics and Analysis published a report stating that in the year 2013 alone there were 4,735 pedestrians killed and 66,000 pedestrians injured in traffic accidents in the United States [8]. A study indicated that at a 40 miles per hour collision there was an 85% chance of death, at 30 miles per hour there was still a 45% chance of death, and at 20 miles per hour there was a significant reduction to a 5% chance of death. This study indicated that the use of automatic emergency braking systems can drastically increase the chance of survival for the pedestrian involved in a traffic accident. One new automatic emergency braking system goes even a step further and combines the use of radar sensors and video cameras, both in the front of the car. This specific system is able to identify and track pedestrians up to 40 meters away. The system also works in crowded urban environments with groups of people and even identifies people using umbrellas in the rain. [9] This last example is still an advancing technology for automakers, but it is great to see that further improvements are being made and that progress continues to go in the right direction of optimizing safety in various traffic situations.
